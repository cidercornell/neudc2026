Overview

Cornell University is proud to host the NEUDC community on our Ithaca, New York campus in 2026. The conference is organized by the Collaboration for International Development Economics Research (CIDER), a cross-campus initiative at Cornell.

NEUDC has organized annual conferences on development economics since 1967, making it the longest-running gathering in the field. The conference rotates annually among its organizing institutions: Boston University, Brown University, Cornell University, Dartmouth College, Harvard University, MIT, Northeastern University, Tufts University, and Yale University.

Call for Papers

Paper Submission Timeline

The submission portal opens on June 15 and closes on July 31. Acceptance decisions will be announced on September 15.

Before submitting, please have the following information ready:

  • Presenter name, title, and institutional affiliation
  • Year PhD was received (or expected)
  • Whether this paper is your job market paper (JMP)
  • Author composition (e.g., faculty only, students only, or a mix)
  • The fields most relevant to your paper (used for reviewer assignment)
